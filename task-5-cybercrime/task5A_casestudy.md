## What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where an attacker pretends to be a trusted
individual or organization — such as a bank, college, or popular website — to
trick the victim into revealing sensitive information like passwords, OTPs,
or banking credentials.

---

## Case Study – The Fake University Portal

Arjun, a first-year engineering student, received an email that appeared to
be from his university's examination department. The email had the
university's logo, a professional layout, and an urgent message stating that
his exam registration would be cancelled unless he logged in and verified his
details within 24 hours.

Panicked, Arjun clicked the link in the email. It took him to a website that
looked exactly like his university portal. He entered his student ID,
password, and even his registered mobile number. A few seconds later, the
page showed an error and asked him to try again.

What Arjun did not know was that the website was a fake — built by an
attacker to look identical to the real portal. His credentials were captured
the moment he hit submit.

## How it Happened – Step by Step

1. Attacker created a fake website mimicking the university portal
2. Sent a mass phishing email to students with an urgent, fear-based message
3. Victim clicked the link without checking the URL carefully
4. Victim entered login credentials on the fake site
5. Attacker captured the credentials in real time
6. Attacker used the stolen login to access the victim's actual university
   account, personal data, and linked email

## Who is Usually Targeted?

- First-year college students who are unfamiliar with official communication
  channels
- People who respond quickly to urgent or threatening messages
- Users who do not verify URLs before entering credentials
- Anyone using the same password across multiple platforms

## Consequences

- Arjun's university account was compromised and his personal data was leaked
- His registered email and phone number were used for further spam attacks
- He had to go through a lengthy account recovery process with the IT department
- His academic records were temporarily inaccessible during the incident
- The psychological stress of the incident affected his focus during exam season
