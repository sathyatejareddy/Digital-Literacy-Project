## General Online Safety

1. **Never click links in unsolicited emails or messages** — always go directly
   to the official website by typing the URL in your browser yourself.

2. **Check the URL carefully before entering any credentials** — fake sites
   often use URLs like "vit-bhopal-portal.com" instead of the real domain.

3. **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all important accounts** —
   email, GitHub, LinkedIn, and banking apps. Even if your password is stolen,
   2FA blocks the attacker.

4. **Never use the same password across multiple platforms** — if one account
   is compromised, all others become vulnerable.

5. **Do not share OTPs with anyone, ever** — no bank, university, or company
   will ever ask for your OTP over call, message, or email.

6. **Verify the sender's email address carefully** — phishing emails often use
   addresses like "support@vit-bhopal.net" instead of the real official domain.

## Financial / UPI Safety

7. **Never share your UPI PIN, CVV, or banking password with anyone** — not
   even someone claiming to be from your bank or payment app support.

8. **Be alert to "money received" scam requests on UPI** — scammers send fake
   collect requests pretending you have received money, tricking you into
   entering your PIN to "accept" it. You never need a PIN to receive money.

## Reporting

9. **Report any cybercrime immediately at cybercrime.gov.in** — the National
   Cyber Crime Reporting Portal is available 24x7 for all types of online fraud.

10. **Call the National Cyber Crime Helpline 1930** — available 24x7, this
    helpline is specifically for reporting financial fraud and cybercrimes
    targeting students and individuals across India.

*Stay alert. Stay safe. When in doubt, don't click.*
