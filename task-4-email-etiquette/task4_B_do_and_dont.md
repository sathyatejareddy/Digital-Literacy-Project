## DO'S

**1. Keep your profiles professional**  
Your GitHub, LinkedIn, and even Instagram can be seen by recruiters and professors. Use a proper name, a decent photo, and a bio that reflects who you are as a student and learner.

**2. Think before you post**  
Once something is online, it is very hard to take back. Before posting anything, ask yourself whether you would be comfortable with a professor, recruiter, or future employer seeing it.

**3. Use social media to learn and network**  
Follow people in your field, join communities related to AI and tech, engage with posts that teach you something new. LinkedIn and Twitter/X are great for this.

**4. Give credit when sharing others' work**  
If you share someone else's project, article, or idea, always mention the original creator. This builds trust and shows integrity.

**5. Report and block harmful content or accounts**  
If you encounter cyberbullying, harassment, or abusive content, use the platform's reporting tools. Do not engage — report and move on.

---

## DON'TS

**1. Don't share personal or sensitive information publicly**  
Never post your phone number, home address, Aadhaar details, UPI IDs, or bank information on any public platform. Scammers actively look for this kind of data.

**2. Don't engage in or ignore cyberbullying**  
Do not bully others online, and do not stay silent when you see it happening. Both participating in it and encouraging it by not reporting can have serious consequences.

**3. Don't post impulsively during emotional moments**  
Posting something out of anger, frustration, or in a heated argument can damage your reputation. Take a breath, step away, and revisit it later.

**4. Don't spread unverified information**  
Before sharing a news article, screenshot, or claim, verify it from a reliable source. Spreading misinformation — even unintentionally — can cause real harm.

**5. Don't use social media as a substitute for real support**  
If you are going through something difficult, reach out to a trusted person or professional. Oversharing personal struggles publicly can be misused or misunderstood.
